Knowing when the user is finished

A compact prosodic classifier distinguishes a real end of turn from an internal pause without hearing any future audio. On grouped English validation it cuts mean delay by 522 ms while respecting the 5% interruption budget.

Problem and causality

At every annotated silence, the live agent must output p_eot: the probability that the user has finished. A false positive can talk over the user; a false negative adds silence. For a decision at pause_start, eot_features.py first takes the single slice x[:floor(pause_start*sr)], and every acoustic calculation operates only on that past slice. The feature API does not accept pause_end or pause duration.

Automated audit: for every one of the 496 supplied annotations, test_causality.py replaces every audio sample at and after pause_start with random noise and asserts that all 136 features remain bit-for-bit identical.

Signals used

Features are calculated over the last 0.3, 0.7, 1.5, and 3.0 seconds, allowing the classifier to contrast the immediate boundary with longer prosodic context.

Model and validation

The final classifier is a soft-probability blend: four parts Extra Trees to one part balanced, standardized logistic regression. Extra Trees captures nonlinear relationships among energy, pitch, and position; logistic regression smooths the boundary. Both languages train together. Five-fold GroupKFold validation groups on language folder plus turn_id, so no pause from a validation turn appears in its training fold.

The final artifact is fitted once on all supplied training turns. predict.py only loads that artifact, extracts causal features, and predicts; it never trains or refits on the target folder.

Error analysis

The largest grouped-validation false-positive scores include en__090 pause 1 (a 1.6 s hold, score 0.774) and hi__085 pause 1 (a 0.5 s hold, score 0.720). Their pre-pause features resemble those of a completed phrase. Low-scored true endings include en__078 pause 0 (0.133) and hi__033 pause 2 (0.137), whose acoustic features appear nonterminal to the model. These errors show the remaining limit of prosody without lexical or conversational context.

Why it beats status quo

A silence-only system gives every pause the same probability and can protect against long internal pauses only by waiting. This model ranks likely endings using information already spoken. On English, that permits a 650 ms action delay for confident decisions and reduces average response delay by 32.6% at the allowed interruption ceiling. On Hindi, it raises AUC from near-random 0.516 to 0.790 even though the discrete scorer’s unusually favorable silence baseline remains tied.
